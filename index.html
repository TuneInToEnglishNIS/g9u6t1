Task 1 :Read the questions and choose A, B, C, or D.

1. In the line "They didn’t know where they was going, but they knew where they was wasn’t it,
" what does "it" likely refer to?
a. Their hometown
b. Their destination
c. Their sense of purpose
d. Their mobile phones

2. What is the central theme of the song “Everyday Robots”?
a. Love and relationships
b. Human-robot interaction
c. The impact of technology on daily life
d. Environmental conservation

3. What does the phrase “everyday robots” in the song imply about people?
a. They are genuinely robotic beings.
b. They are controlled by technology and routine.
c. They have superhuman abilities.
d. They are disconnected from the world.

4. What does the song suggest about the use of mobile phones?
a. Mobile phones enhance human connections.
b. Mobile phones have no impact on daily life.
c. Mobile phone usage can lead to isolation.
d. Mobile phones are primarily used for work.

5. The line “Swimming in lingo they become” suggests that people are:
a. Engaging in water sports.
b. Mastering a new language.
c. Immersed in technology-related language.
d. Isolated from society.

6. What emotions or feelings are associated with “everyday robots getting old” in the song?
a. A sense of youth and vitality
b. Monotony and weariness
c. Joy and excitement
d. Fear of the unknown
